Tracking Knowledge and Idenity

In our innaugural issue we want to collect tales of methodology. How are you tracking knowledge and identity in your social media work? Are you using semantic data to build graphs? traditional cotent analysis? Digital or autoethnogaphy?, the real kind not the lip service version.

We will want more than "This method of analysis is rooted in "idenity kits" (Gee, incorrect page number)," or "constuctivism (Random Piaget and Vygotsky citations, probably one a version in Russion you can't read." As we think about or describe the methods dig deep into underlying belief systems. Think about who benfits in these systems? Be critical of your your own perspective.

Then describe methods you have used in the past in context of your theory. You may use theory to critique a method commonly used to today. Finally you can submit theoretical methodologies based in theory that are applicable today. In other words, unless a piece on futurism, don't describe a theoretical study relying on autmoated tutorial dog-bots shaped like kittens.

What's nice about the web is that it looks unfinished

You should see a common theme. We want to use theory to explore existing and possible methods for tracing idenity and knowledge. I think we have said theory enough to get the point across. Of course if yoru theory is there is no theory you are welcome to unexplain the methods we can not use as well.

Submissions can be of any length. They just must be from your own website or blog. If you don't kknow any html do not worry. You can just publish your work as normal. When the piece is done your facilitators will help you syndicate to our site.

If you do know html/css youare welcome to style and write your article in any kind of modality you choose. No javascript please...Welcome to use on your own site but we don't want to worry about all that here.
